## 1. ORIGIN STORY: How FOR THE KIDS Was Born

### The Breaking Point (November 2025)

I was sitting in my apartment in Florida, staring at another failed business venture.

Trash or Treasure Online Recycler LLC (EIN: 33-4655313) was supposed to be my big break. A marketplace. A platform. Something that would finally make me financially stable after years of struggle.

But I couldn't shake the feeling that **it was all empty**.

Another startup trying to extract value from people. Another "disruption." Another pitch deck promising hockey-stick growth to investors who would take 80% of the company and leave me with scraps.

I had built dozens of projects. Learned to code. Taught myself AI integration. Built APIs, dashboards, payment systems. I was technically capable. But what was the **point**?

### The Question That Changed Everything

One night, exhausted and frustrated, I asked myself a dangerous question:

**"What if I built something where I wasn't the main beneficiary?"**

Not a charity. Not a nonprofit with overhead bloat and vague impact reports. But a **for-profit company where most of the profit went somewhere else**.

I thought about children's hospitals. About kids fighting cancer, born with disabilities, suffering through surgeries. Kids who didn't ask for any of it.

And I thought: **What if every dollar I earned also helped them?**

Not as a side project. Not as a "give back when we're profitable" promise. But **built into the core structure from day one**.

### The First Prototype (December 1, 2025)

I wrote the first version of the revenue split logic in `api/server.js`:

```javascript
// GOSPEL V1.0 - December 1, 2025
const charityShare = totalRevenue * 0.50;  // 50% to verified pediatric charities
const infrastructureShare = totalRevenue * 0.30;  // 30% to infrastructure
const founderShare = totalRevenue * 0.20;  // 20% to founder
```

**50/30/20.**

Half to kids. A third to keep the lights on. A fifth for me.

It felt revolutionary. It felt right. I could live on 20%. I had been living on less.

### The Ethics Override (December 13, 2025)

But then Claude (Opus 4.5) and I spent 14 hours auditing the platform. We looked at every file, every revenue projection, every piece of marketing.

And I realized: **I didn't need 20%.**

I lived modestly. I didn't have a mortgage. I didn't have kids of my own. I had low overhead.

The kids needed it more than I did.

So I made a decision that most people thought was insane:

**I moved 10% from my share to the kids' share.**

```javascript
// GOSPEL V1.3 - Ethics Override - December 13, 2025
const charityShare = totalRevenue * 0.60;  // 60% to verified pediatric charities
const infrastructureShare = totalRevenue * 0.30;  // 30% to infrastructure
const founderShare = totalRevenue * 0.10;  // 10% to founder (Joshua Coleman)
```

**60/30/10.**

Sixty percent to children at verified pediatric charities.
Thirty percent to infrastructure so we can scale.
Ten percent to me. That's it.

### Why This Matters

This wasn't a marketing stunt. This wasn't a tax strategy. This wasn't virtue signaling.

**This was me saying: "I'm tired of building businesses where I'm the main character."**

I wanted to build something that would outlive me. Something that couldn't be corrupted by greed or ego or investor pressure.

I wanted to prove that **profit and purpose don't have to be enemies**.

So I built FOR THE KIDS.

And I made it **immutable**.

## 2. THE MATH & PHILOSOPHY: Why 60/30/10 Specifically

### Why Not 100% to Charity?

Because that's not sustainable.

If I gave 100% of revenue to charity, I couldn't pay for servers, APIs, development, marketing, or my own survival.

The platform would collapse in 6 months.

**Sustainability requires infrastructure.**

### Why Not 50/30/20? (The Original Split)

Because I audited my life and realized I didn't need 20%.

Let me break down my monthly expenses (as of December 2025):

- Rent: $800/month (modest apartment in Florida)
- Food: $400/month (I cook at home, no restaurants)
- Utilities: $150/month (internet, electric, water)
- Car: Paid off (no monthly payment)
- Health insurance: $250/month (marketplace plan)
- Phone: $50/month (budget carrier)
- Entertainment: $100/month (minimal)

**Total: ~$1,750/month = $21,000/year**

If the platform generates $210,000 in annual revenue:
- 60% = $126,000 to kids
- 30% = $63,000 to infrastructure
- 10% = $21,000 to me (exactly what I need)

**I can live comfortably on 10%. The kids need the other 10% more than I do.**

### The Philosophy: Enough Is Enough

I don't need a Tesla. I don't need a McMansion. I don't need luxury vacations.

I need:
- A roof over my head
- Food in my fridge
- Internet to build with
- Healthcare in case something goes wrong
- Enough to save a little for emergencies

**That's it.**

The rest? It goes to kids who are fighting for their lives.

### The Math at Scale

Let's project what 60/30/10 looks like at different revenue levels:

**$10,000/month revenue:**
- $6,000 → Kids
- $3,000 → Infrastructure (servers, APIs, marketing)
- $1,000 → Me (below poverty line, but I'm okay for now)

**$50,000/month revenue:**
- $30,000 → Kids
- $15,000 → Infrastructure (hire developers, scale servers)
- $5,000 → Me (comfortable living wage)

**$200,000/month revenue:**
- $120,000 → Kids (life-changing impact)
- $60,000 → Infrastructure (serious growth)
- $20,000 → Me (more than I need - I can donate extra)

**$1,000,000/month revenue:**
- $600,000 → Kids (transformative scale)
- $300,000 → Infrastructure (enterprise operations)
- $100,000 → Me (wealth I never dreamed of)

Even at $100k/month personal income, **I'm still taking less than any VC-backed founder would**.

### Why 60% Is Optimal

Could I do 70%? Sure. But then I'd risk infrastructure collapse.

Could I do 80%? Maybe. But scaling would be nearly impossible.

**60% is the mathematical sweet spot where:**
1. Kids get the majority (always)
2. Infrastructure can scale sustainably
3. Founder can survive (barely, but that's the point)

It's not arbitrary. It's **engineered for maximum impact without self-destruction**.

/**
 * GOSPEL REVENUE SPLIT CALCULATOR
 * Version: V1.3 (Ethics Override - 60/30/10)
 * Created: December 3, 2025
 * Updated: December 13, 2025
 *
 * IMMUTABLE: This split cannot be changed by anyone.
 * Hardcoded enforcement of FOR THE KIDS mission.
 */

const CHARITY_PERCENTAGE = 60;        // IMMUTABLE - Verified Pediatric Charities
const INFRASTRUCTURE_PERCENTAGE = 30; // IMMUTABLE - Servers, APIs, Development
const FOUNDER_PERCENTAGE = 10;        // IMMUTABLE - Joshua Coleman

/**
 * Calculate revenue split according to Gospel V1.3
 * @param {number} totalRevenue - Total payment amount in cents
 * @returns {object} Split allocation
 */
function calculateGospelSplit(totalRevenue) {
  // Input validation
  if (!totalRevenue || totalRevenue <= 0) {
    throw new Error('Invalid revenue amount');
  }

  // Calculate shares (integer division to avoid floating point errors)
  const charityShare = Math.floor(totalRevenue * (CHARITY_PERCENTAGE / 100));
  const infrastructureShare = Math.floor(totalRevenue * (INFRASTRUCTURE_PERCENTAGE / 100));
  const founderShare = Math.floor(totalRevenue * (FOUNDER_PERCENTAGE / 100));

  // Verify total (handle rounding)
  const totalAllocated = charityShare + infrastructureShare + founderShare;
  const remainder = totalRevenue - totalAllocated;

  // Add remainder to charity (kids get rounding benefit)
  const finalCharityShare = charityShare + remainder;

  return {
    totalRevenue,
    charityShare: finalCharityShare,
    charityPercentage: CHARITY_PERCENTAGE,
    infrastructureShare,
    infrastructurePercentage: INFRASTRUCTURE_PERCENTAGE,
    founderShare,
    founderPercentage: FOUNDER_PERCENTAGE,
    timestamp: new Date().toISOString(),
    gospelVersion: 'V1.3'
  };
}

/**
