## Content Performance Framework

### High-Engagement Elements (Keep These)

1. **Specific Numbers**
   - ROI metrics (94% cost reduction, 340% engagement increase)
   - Time savings (15 hours → 2 hours)
   - Revenue impact ($47K MRR added)
   - Cost comparisons ($299 vs. $8K/month)

2. **Real-World Examples**
   - "SaaS company with 2,000 users..."
   - "B2B consulting firm went from..."
   - "Operations director at 50-person agency..."
   - "Manufacturing client needed AI quality inspection..."

3. **Question Headlines**
   - "What If Your Business Had a Chief of Staff That Never Sleeps?"
   - Triggers curiosity and engagement

4. **Problem → Solution Structure**
   - Pain point first (relatable)
   - Solution introduction (product)
   - Features and benefits (clear value)
   - Proof (case study/numbers)

### Low-Engagement Elements (Avoid These)

1. **Generic Claims**
   - "Industry-leading solution"
   - "Revolutionary technology"
   - "Best in class"

2. **Vague Benefits**
   - "Improve efficiency" (by how much?)
   - "Save time" (how much time?)
   - "Increase revenue" (specific numbers?)

3. **Feature Dumping**
   - Long lists without context
   - Technical jargon without business impact
   - Features without ROI connection

4. **Weak CTAs**
   - "Check it out"
   - "Learn more" (without clear next step)
   - No CTA at all
