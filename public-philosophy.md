## My Story (Why This Matters)

I'm a car accident survivor. I have titanium plates a centimeter from my spinal cord. 

I take care of my brother - he's around 40 years old physically, but mentally he's about 12-14. He's a kid in need who didn't ask for his circumstances.

I'm an electrician by trade. I'm not a developer. I'm not a tech entrepreneur. I'm someone who looked at AI and thought: "Maybe this can help me help kids who didn't ask for what they're dealing with."

That's the whole origin story.

## The Philosophy: NEED vs. HAVE

Here's what keeps me up at night:

A kid needs surgery. Medicine. Treatment. Care.

Why do they NEED it? Because humanity hasn't already provided it. Because somewhere, someone decided profits matter more than having that medicine already in stock, waiting, in case a kid needed it.

Kids shouldn't NEED things. They should already HAVE them.

I don't care about the rich man's rebuttal. I don't care about his justification for why he can't help. The moment he thinks he has a "reason" not to help a sick kid, he's already lost the argument in my book. Period.

Mathematically or ethically, it will never make sense to me that a child's health depends on whether someone feels like helping.

## Priority: Medical First

The 60% goes to verified children's hospitals. MEDICAL priority.

**Hierarchy:**
1. Medical needs (surgery, medicine, treatments, health) - FIRST
2. School supplies, backpacks, shoes - important, but AFTER medical
3. When no child needs medical help, expand to other needs

No specific charity partnerships are claimed without official agreements. The goal: make sure no child lacks medical care, then expand to ensure no child lacks anything.

## Business First, Charity Silent

**Critical distinction:**

### Transparency (Public)
- Full codebase visibility
- Blockchain-enforced split
- Auditable financials
- Open source everything

### Marketing (Public)
- **Pure product focus**
- NO charity mentions
- NO "buy to help kids"
- NO guilt-based selling
- Products sell on merit alone

### The Reveal (Customer Only)
- **Receipt/Invoice ONLY** shows the 60/30/10 breakdown
- Acts as a "Thank You" note
- Customer learns AFTER purchase that they helped

### Why?
The "charity scam" skepticism is real. People distrust "we donate X%" claims. By selling the product on its own merits:
1. We avoid skepticism entirely
2. The product must actually be good
3. Revenue flows regardless of charity messaging
4. The Gospel handles charity automatically

**More ruthless at business = more money for kids.**

The receipt becomes a pleasant surprise, not a sales pitch.
