# SECTION 12: A/B TEST RECOMMENDATIONS

## Email Component Testing Priority

### Priority 1: Subject Lines (Test Every Campaign)
**Why:** 80% of email success depends on subject line
**Method:** A/B test 2 variants, 50/50 split, min 1,000 recipients
**Win Criteria:** Highest open rate + conversion rate (not just opens)

**Test Variants:**
- Mission-first vs. Value-first
- Emotional vs. Data-driven
- Short (5 words) vs. Long (10+ words)
- Question vs. Statement
- Urgency vs. No urgency

---

### Priority 2: Call-to-Action Placement
**Why:** CTA location dramatically affects click rates
**Method:** Test CTA at top vs. middle vs. bottom of email
**Win Criteria:** Highest click-through rate

**Test Variants:**
- Single CTA at bottom (control)
- CTA at top + bottom (repeat)
- CTA in middle + bottom
- Inline text links throughout

---

### Priority 3: Email Length
**Why:** Some audiences prefer short, others prefer detailed
**Method:** Short version (200 words) vs. Long version (500+ words)
**Win Criteria:** Highest conversion rate (not just opens)

**Test Variants:**
- Short: 3 paragraphs, 1 CTA
- Medium: 5 paragraphs, 2 CTAs
- Long: 8+ paragraphs, multiple CTAs

---

### Priority 4: Tone and Voice
**Why:** Different audiences respond to different messaging styles
**Method:** Technical vs. Emotional vs. Balanced tone
**Win Criteria:** Highest engagement (open + click + conversion)

**Test Variants:**
- Technical: Data-driven, blockchain proof, smart contract
- Emotional: Children's stories, impact narratives, founder story
- Balanced: Mix of both

---

### Priority 5: Personalization Level
**Why:** More personalization can increase engagement or feel creepy
**Method:** No personalization vs. Name only vs. Behavioral data
**Win Criteria:** Highest conversion rate

**Test Variants:**
- Generic: "Hey there"
- Name: "Hey [First Name]"
- Behavioral: "Hey [First Name], you've sent {{queries}} queries this month"
