## Why I Built This (Bootstrapped Founder Story)

I'm a solo founder. I had $25K in savings and a choice:

**Option A:** Take the conventional founder path (30% allocation, raise VC, scale fast, "add ethics later")

**Option B:** Cap my take at 10%, give 60% to kids, bootstrap, and force myself to build lean

I chose Option B. Here's why it's actually better:

**1. No Excuses for Bad Unit Economics**

With 30% founder allocation, I could waste money. With 10%, every inefficiency hit my personal runway. Result: I built the most efficient infrastructure possible on my first try.

**2. Team Quality Went Up Immediately**

When I posted "We're hiring, 60/30/10 model," I got two types:
- Type A: "That's stupid, fix it" (didn't hire)
- Type B: "Wait, you're actually doing this? Can I work here?" (hired these)

Team members took lower pay for alignment. They execute better than any market-rate team I've had.

**3. Customer Selection Got Clearer**

With 10% founder take and 60% to charities, I got selective:
- Do customers understand the model?
- Will they stay? (High LTV because they chose us for the model)
- Can they help improve governance?

Paradox: Being selective made growth faster.

**4. I Stopped Lying to Myself About Margins**

The allocation happens automatically. I either make enough to cover it, or I don't. This forced me to face real unit economics by Month 3, not Year 3.

## The Uncomfortable Truth

**This only works if you actually mean it.**

If you're doing 60/30/10 as marketing while planning to "adjust later," everyone can tell. Builders smell that instantly.

Only do this if:
1. You're genuinely okay living on less
2. You're willing to make it structural (code, not promises)
3. You're willing to fail rather than break the commitment
4. You're motivated by something other than money

If any of those is false, don't do this. Just optimize for profit. It's cleaner.
