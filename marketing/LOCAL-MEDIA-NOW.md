**GAINESVILLE, FL - December 17, 2025** - A Gainesville-area tech entrepreneur is proving that Florida innovation can compete with Silicon Valley - not by chasing billion-dollar valuations, but by hardcoding charitable giving into mathematics.

Joshua Coleman, founder of FOR THE KIDS (https://aidoesitall.website), has built an AI-powered automation platform where 60% of all profits automatically flow to verified pediatric charities through a blockchain smart contract deployed on Base Mainnet. The mission isn't a marketing pledge - it's immutable code.

"I grew up in this area. I know what it means when families can't afford pediatric care," said Coleman. "I built this platform to prove you don't have to choose between building a real business and helping kids. You can do both - and you can prove it with blockchain."

### THE REVENUE SPLIT (HARDCODED)

The platform operates under Gospel V1.3, an ethical framework enforced at the code level:

```
60% → Verified Pediatric Charities (including potential partnership with UF Health Shands Children's Hospital)
30% → Infrastructure & Platform Sustainability
10% → Founder Compensation
```

The smart contract address (planned for Q1 2026, backend allocation currently active) is publicly verifiable on Base blockchain. Anyone can confirm that the 60% allocation happens automatically, transparently, and immutably.

"I actually reduced my own share from 20% to 10% last month," Coleman explains. "I took less so kids get more. And I wrote it into code so I can never reverse that decision - even if investors pressure me, even if the business scales to millions."

### UNIVERSITY OF FLORIDA CONNECTION

Coleman is exploring partnerships with UF Health Shands Children's Hospital as a primary beneficiary of the 60% allocation. The platform could potentially provide AI automation tools to UF students, researchers, and faculty - with every dollar of profit flowing back to pediatric care.

"Imagine UF students using AI tools built in Gainesville, knowing 60% of every dollar goes to children's healthcare at Shands," Coleman notes. "That's a closed loop of local impact."

The platform is also in discussions with UF's Computer Science and AI research programs as a case study in ethical AI development and blockchain transparency.

### ALL CODE WRITTEN BY AI (NO HUMAN SHORTCUTS)

In a novel approach to mission enforcement, Coleman built the entire platform using Claude AI from Anthropic. Every line of code follows immutable "Gospel principles" that prevent the AI from generating code that violates the 60% allocation.

"Human developers get tired, pressured, or distracted. Claude doesn't," Coleman says. "If I enforce the mission at the code generation layer, the mission becomes unbreakable."

Every git commit includes: `Co-Authored-By: Claude <noreply@anthropic.com>` as proof of AI authorship and mission compliance.

### FLORIDA TECH, FLORIDA VALUES

While Silicon Valley startups chase venture capital and explosive growth, FOR THE KIDS embraces what Coleman calls "the anti-unicorn model":

- No dilution of 60% charity allocation through funding rounds
- No pressure to pivot when profits scale
- Infinite timeline (not optimized for 7-year exit)
- Infrastructure runs on recycled enterprise servers (minimizing costs to maximize charity flow)

"We're not trying to be valued at $1 billion," Coleman states. "We're trying to run for 100 years. Gainesville taught me that sustainability matters more than flash."

### VERIFICATION AND TRANSPARENCY

The smart contract is deployed on Base blockchain with full public verification. Anyone can:

- View real-time transaction history
- Confirm the 60/30/10 split is hardcoded
- Verify charity recipients are legitimate 501(c)(3) organizations
- Track exact dollar amounts flowing to each beneficiary
