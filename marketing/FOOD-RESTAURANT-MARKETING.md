# Food & Restaurant Industry Marketing Strategy
## FOR THE KIDS - Campaign URL: https://aidoesitall.website

**Mission:** 60% of all revenue to Verified Pediatric Charities (Gospel V1.3)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Food brings people together for good. The food and restaurant industry represents a multi-billion dollar opportunity for FOR THE KIDS to embed charity into everyday dining decisions. This comprehensive strategy outlines 12 integrated campaigns to build deep partnerships across restaurants, food services, suppliers, and consumers.

**Target Revenue Impact:** $500K - $2M annually from food & restaurant partnerships

---

## 1. RESTAURANT CHARITY NIGHT PROGRAM

### Program Overview
Partner with restaurants to host monthly "Dine for the Kids" nights where a portion of sales benefits FOR THE KIDS pediatric charities.

### Implementation
- **Partner Recruitment:** Target 500+ restaurants nationally (independent and chains)
- **Hosting Platform:** Create dedicated landing page at `aidoesitall.website/charity-nights`
- **Marketing Materials:**
  - Pre-designed table tents (QR code → campaign page)
  - Digital posters for restaurants
  - Email templates for customer lists
  - Social media graphics (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)

### Revenue Model
- Restaurants contribute 10-20% of dinner sales
- FOR THE KIDS keeps 60% of contributions (Gospel V1.3)
- Restaurants keep 40% as marketing/goodwill expense
- Average per event: $500-$2,000 based on restaurant size

### Launch Strategy
- Week 1: Pilot with 10 high-traffic restaurants in major cities
- Week 2-3: Expand to 50 restaurants
- Month 2+: National rollout with franchise support
- Target: 250 active restaurants within 6 months

### Success Metrics
- Number of participating restaurants
- Average contribution per event ($)
- Customer participation rate (%)
- Social media engagement

### Implementation Phases
**Phase 1 (Month 1-2):** DoorDash & Uber Eats pilot (50 cities)
**Phase 2 (Month 3):** Add Grubhub integration
**Phase 3 (Month 4):** Instacart integration for grocery contributions

### Revenue Projection

- **Seasons:** Minimum 3-season commitment

### Revenue Model
- Network licensing fee (advance for production)

## 8. COFFEE SHOP ROUND-UP PROGRAM

### Concept Overview
"Round-Up for the Kids" - Customers round their purchase to next dollar, spare change benefits pediatric charities.

### Implementation

#### Partner Recruitment
- **National Chains:** Starbucks, Dunkin', local independent shops
- **Target:** 5,000+ locations within 12 months
- **Pitch:** Simple to implement, low operational burden

#### Point-of-Sale Integration
- **Hardware:** QR code displays or integrated POS screens
- **Software:** White-label solution connecting to FOR THE KIDS platform
- **Training:** 30-minute barista training program

#### Customer Experience
1. Order coffee as normal
2. Cashier/POS screen shows: "Round up to $X for pediatric charities?"
3. Customer approves with tap, card, or app
4. Receipt shows contribution amount + impact
5. Monthly email: "Your coffee funded..." (specific charity impact)

#### Incentive Structure
- **Customer:** Name entered in monthly $50 coffee gift card drawing
- **Barista:** Quarterly bonuses for top-performing locations
- **Store:** Monthly marketing highlighting participation

### Marketing Campaign

**In-Store Materials:**
- Branded contribution cards at register
- Menu board stickers: "Round-Up for the Kids"
- Window decals showing cumulative contributions
- Barista apron patches

**Social Media:**
- #RoundUpForTheKids campaign
- Customer testimonial videos (30-60 seconds)
- Daily posts showing small contributions adding up
- Monthly "coffee hero" spotlight

**Email Marketing:**
- Monthly impact reports to participating customers
- contribution milestones ("We've raised $100K!")
- Charity partner spotlights

### Revenue Projection
- 5,000 locations with average 300 transactions/day
- 8-12% round-up participation rate = 120K-180K daily transactions
- Average round-up: $0.50
- Daily revenue: $60K-$90K
- Monthly: $1.8M-$2.7M
- FOR THE KIDS share (60%): $1.08M-$1.62M/month

**This is the highest-volume revenue opportunity in the portfolio.**

## 11. RESTAURANT WEEK CHARITY TIE-IN

### Program Overview
Leverage existing "Restaurant Week" events in 75+ US cities to drive FOR THE KIDS engagement.

### Campaign Structure

#### Restaurant Week Mechanics
- Typically occurs once per quarter in major cities
- Fixed-price menus ($25, $35, $50 per person)
- 50+ participating restaurants per city
- 500K+ diners annually across all cities

#### FOR THE KIDS Integration
- **Special Menu Items:** Restaurants create 1 "Kids' Table" signature dish
- **Pricing Structure:** Regular price + $2.50 FOR THE KIDS contribution
- **Marketing:** Highlight FOR THE KIDS in Restaurant Week promotions
- **Promotion:** Featured in Restaurant Week app/website
- **Social Media:** Cross-promotion with Restaurant Week accounts

### Campaign Elements

**Pre-Restaurant Week (2 weeks before):**
- Launch email campaign to restaurant list
- Provide marketing collateral (posters, digital assets)
- Host virtual training for restaurant staff
- Send pre-written social media posts

**During Restaurant Week:**
- Daily social media posts featuring participating restaurants
- Instagram Stories takeovers from partner chefs
- Highlight contribution milestones
- Real-time impact metrics ("$50K raised so far!")

**Post-Restaurant Week:**
- Thank-you emails to diners with impact report
- Detailed metrics shared with restaurants
- Press release highlighting total raised
- Momentum carried forward to next week event

### City Strategy

**Tier 1 Cities (10 cities) - Major Investment:**
- New York, LA, Chicago, San Francisco, Miami
- Houston, Dallas, Boston, Washington DC, Austin
- Target: 100+ restaurants per city

**Tier 2 Cities (25 cities) - Standard Program:**
- Mid-size metros with established Restaurant Week
- Denver, Nashville, Phoenix, Atlanta, etc.
- Target: 50+ restaurants per city

**Tier 3 Cities (40 cities) - Growth Program:**
- Emerging cities with new Restaurant Week events
- Target: 20+ restaurants per city

### Revenue Projection
- 75 cities × 50 restaurants × 20 diners/week × $2.50 contribution = $187.5K/week

- 75 × 1.5 weeks = 112.5 weeks annually
