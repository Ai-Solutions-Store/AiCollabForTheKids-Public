## CO-MARKETING FRAMEWORK

### What Is Co-Marketing?

Co-marketing is a partnership where two organizations share resources, audiences, and effort to promote mutual benefit.

**FOR THE KIDS Model:**
```
FOR THE KIDS + [Partner] = Mutual Brand Lift

FOR THE KIDS Gets:
+- Audience access
+- Credibility boost
+- Content creation
+- Distribution amplification

Partner Gets:
+- Mission alignment
+- Customer loyalty
+- Community recognition
+- Content collaboration
```

### Key Principle

**"Neither party pays the other. Both parties benefit equally from the partnership."**

This is fundamentally different from advertising, where one party pays another.

### Why Co-Marketing Works

**For Businesses:**
- Demonstrates values alignment
- Provides content they need to create anyway
- Reaches new audiences
- Builds brand loyalty
- Zero cost or low cost

**For FOR THE KIDS:**
- Expands reach exponentially
- Builds credibility through association
- Gets professional marketing assets
- Reaches target audiences efficiently
- Reduces customer acquisition cost

### Co-Marketing Win-Win Examples

**Pizza Shop + FOR THE KIDS:**
- Pizza shop reaches new customers (mission-driven)
- FOR THE KIDS reaches foodie/local community
- Both benefit from co-branded social media
- Both benefit from word of mouth

**Coffee Shop + FOR THE KIDS:**
- Coffee shop highlights ethical business model
- FOR THE KIDS reaches daily customers
- Joint loyalty program benefits both
- Co-created content (Instagram stories, etc.)

**Nonprofit Partner + FOR THE KIDS:**
- Nonprofit reaches new audiences
- FOR THE KIDS reaches values-aligned community
- Joint fundraising opportunity
- Cross-promotion to all supporters

## BUSINESS PARTNER CO-MARKETING

### Model 1: Social Media Cross-Promotion

#### What You Provide

**FOR THE KIDS:**
- Monthly social media content (5-10 posts)
- Business-specific stories
- Customer testimonials
- Impact content

**Business Partner:**
- Reposting on their channels
- Sharing with their audience
- Using provided graphics
- Tagging FOR THE KIDS in content

#### Sample Co-Marketing Posts

**Business Partner Perspective:**

```
"We're proud to partner with FOR THE KIDS!

When you buy from us, you're not just getting great [product/service].
You're automatically supporting pediatric charities.

60% of our [specific product] revenue goes to kids' medical care.
Locked by smart contract. Completely transparent.

Your [product] purchase = healthcare for a child somewhere.

That's how we do business. ??

Learn more: aidoesitall.website

#ForTheKids #SupportLocal #ChildHealth"
```

**FOR THE KIDS Perspective:**

```
"Meet [Business Name]! ??

[Owner Name] decided to change how [their business] works.
60% of [specific service] revenue now goes to pediatric charities.

Why? Because business can be force for good.

If you need [service], [Business Name] is now an option where your purchase helps kids.

[Link to business page]

Thank you, [Business Name], for doing good.

#ForTheKids #GoodBusiness #CommunityPartner"
```

#### Implementation

**Monthly Cadence:**
- Week 1: Customer story
- Week 2: Impact update
- Week 3: Business spotlight
- Week 4: Partnership announcement or seasonal

**Partnership Agreement:**
- Business commits to sharing 80% of posts provided
- FOR THE KIDS provides content monthly
- Both tag and credit each other
- 12-month minimum commitment

**ROI for Business:**
- 3-5 new customers per month from cross-promotion
- Content creation handled (saves time/money)
- Brand positioning as values-aligned
- Organic reach amplification

## NONPROFIT PARTNERSHIP MARKETING

### Model 1: Mutual Audience Cross-Promotion

#### Alignment

**Ideal Nonprofit Partners:**
- Children's hospitals
- Pediatric medical charities
- Kids health organizations
- Community child welfare groups

**NOT ideal:**
- Charities that provide direct fundraising (competing for dollars)
- Charities with different values
- Charities with existing corporate sponsors asking for exclusivity

#### Co-Marketing Strategy

**Phase 1: Introduction**

```
Email to nonprofit director:
"We're a business that allocates 60% of revenue to verified
pediatric charities. We'd like to partner with [nonprofit].

Partnership idea: Mutual promotion to our audiences.
We reach business owners and customers.
You reach supporters and supporters.
Together we expand both audiences."
```

**Phase 2: Content Exchange**

**Nonprofit Provides:**
- Stories about kids helped
- Impact data and statistics
- Testimonials from families
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Educational materials about healthcare needs

**FOR THE KIDS Provides:**
- Revenue allocation data
- Business partnership stories
- Customer testimonials
- Growth metrics
- Sustainability model explanation

**Phase 3: Joint Campaigns**

**Campaign Ideas:**
1. "Business Supporting Medicine" feature series
2. "One Purchase, One Child" impact campaign
3. "Healthcare Access Through Business" educational series
4. Annual "Corporate Partners for Kids" celebration

#### Implementation

**Content Calendar:**
- Monthly webinar featuring nonprofit
- Quarterly impact report co-authored
- Weekly social media cross-promotion
- Annual joint event

**Distribution:**
- Nonprofit newsletter features FOR THE KIDS
- FOR THE KIDS newsletter features nonprofit
- Both social media accounts cross-promote
- Joint email campaigns to combined audiences
