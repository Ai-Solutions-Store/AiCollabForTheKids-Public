**Long Answer:**
"Trust is earned, not given. That's why we built FOR THE KIDS on blockchain�it's Transparent by design. Here's what that means:

1. **Every transaction is public**: You can verify where your money went, when, and to whom.
2. **backend systems enforce the split**: The 60/30/10 model isn't a policy�it's code. I can't override it.

### Q4: "What if the kids don't actually benefit?"

**Short Answer:**
"We verify charities before, during, and after. If they don't deliver, we remove them and reallocate funds."

**Long Answer:**
"This is the right question to ask, and here's how we ensure impact:

**Pre-Verification (Before They Join):**
- Charity Navigator ratings (4-star minimum)
- Financial audits (IRS 990 forms)
- Track record of pediatric impact
- Legal compliance (verified charity partnership)

**Continuous Verification (During Partnership):**
- AI monitoring of financial reports
- Outcome tracking (kids helped, services delivered)
- supporter feedback loops
- Real-time blockchain auditing

**Post-Verification (Ongoing Accountability):**
- Annual re-certification required
- Community reporting mechanisms
- Transparent removal process if standards slip

If a charity fails verification, we pause funding immediately, notify supporters, and reallocate their future contributions to verified alternatives. You'll be notified, and you can choose where your money goes instead.

We're not perfect, but we're accountable. And that's what separates us from the status quo."
