## QUICK START GUIDE (30 MINUTES)

### Step 1: Read the Quick Reference (5 minutes)
Open `PARTNERSHIP-QUICK-REFERENCE.md` and skim:
- The 5 partnership types
- Key talking points
- Email structure

### Step 2: Choose Your First Target Category (2 minutes)
Pick ONE category to start with:
- **Children's Hospitals** (if you want to secure primary charity partner)
- **Tech Companies** (if you want sponsorship/co-marketing)
- **Charity Platforms** (if you want cross-promotion)
- **Corporate CSR** (if you want bulk revenue fast)
- **Local Businesses** (if you want community support)

### Step 3: Research 5 Prospects (15 minutes total = 3 min each)
For each prospect:
1. Find company website + LinkedIn profile
2. Note recent news/awards/launches
3. Identify contact person (partnerships, BD, CSR, owner)
4. Write down 1 specific reason to reach out

### Step 4: Personalize Template (5 minutes per email)
1. Open the relevant template from `PARTNERSHIP-OUTREACH-TEMPLATES.md`
2. Replace ALL [PLACEHOLDERS] with your research
3. Add 1-2 sentences unique to this prospect
4. Proofread for [BRACKETS] you missed

### Step 5: Send + Track (3 minutes)
1. Send email (best time: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM or 2-4 PM)
2. Add to tracking spreadsheet (`PARTNERSHIP-TRACKING-TEMPLATE.md`)
3. Set reminder for follow-up (5-7 days)

**Total Time: 30 minutes for 5 personalized emails**

## GOSPEL V1.3 COMPLIANCE RULES

**ALWAYS SAY:**
- "60% of revenue" (NEVER "60%")
- "Allocate" or "revenue split" (NEVER "contribute" or "treasury")
- "Verified pediatric charities" (NEVER "charity" without "pediatric")
- "Gospel V1.3" (NEVER "V1.2" or older)
- "60/30/10 split" (NEVER "60/30/10")

**NEVER SAY:**
- "contribute" (we're not soliciting contributions)
- "treasury" (violates Corporate Sovereign model)
- "revenue allocation" (it's a business expense, not a contribution)
- "60% to charity" (outdated Gospel V1.3 language)
- "20% to founder" (outdated Gospel V1.3 language)

**WHY THIS MATTERS:**
Gospel V1.3 represents the Ethics Override where the Founder voluntarily reduced his share from 20% to 10% so kids get 60% instead of 50%. Using old language (60/30/10) erases this sacrifice and violates the mission.

## COMMON OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

### "We already have a charity partner"
**Response:**
> "That's great�we're not asking to replace your current giving. FOR THE KIDS is about turning operational spend (software purchases) into additional charitable impact. Different budget, different mechanism. But if now's not the right time, totally understand."

### "How do we know the money actually goes to charity?"
**Response:**
> "Three layers of transparency: First, our smart contract on Base blockchain enforces the 60% split�it's mathematically impossible for us to change. Second, we have a public dashboard showing every transaction in real-time. Third, we provide quarterly impact reports with exact allocation amounts. You can verify everything yourself."

### "Your pricing is too high"
**Response:**
> "We keep pricing consistent because discounting reduces the 60% to charity. However, we do offer extended trials (60-90 days) so you can fully evaluate before committing. If you find value, the purchase funds children's healthcare. If not, no commitment. Fair?"

### "Can you guarantee which hospital receives our payment?"
**Response:**
> "We're currently finalizing our primary hospital partnership (expected by Month 6). Once signed, 100% of the 60% charity allocation goes to that verified partner. Until then, funds are held in a safe harbor ledger. We're talking with [Hospital Names] right now. Would you like updates on which partner we choose?"

### "This sounds too good to be true"
**Response:**
> "I get it�60% to charity is unusual. Here's why it works: our BYOK (Bring Your Own Keys) model means customers pay their own AI costs directly to OpenAI/Anthropic/etc. We just charge for the software license, which has 95%+ margins. So we can afford to allocate 60% and still sustain the platform. It's not charity; it's smart business model design."

### "Why not 100% to charity then?"
**Response:**
> "Because 30% goes to infrastructure (servers, APIs, development) and 10% provides sustainable founder income. If we gave 100%, the platform would collapse within months. The 60/30/10 split is the maximum sustainable allocation�it's the sweet spot between impact and longevity. We'd rather give 60% forever than 100% for six months."
