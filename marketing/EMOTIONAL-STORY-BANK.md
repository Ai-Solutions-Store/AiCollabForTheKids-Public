"I'm a founder who took a 60% pay cut�voluntarily. Not because I had to. Because I realized something: my kids are fine. But millions aren't. So I moved 10% of my share to theirs. **Medium Version (2 minutes):**
"I built FOR THE KIDS after a moment that changed everything. I was working on another AI project, watching revenue grow, planning my next move. Then I saw my daughter playing�healthy, safe, loved. And I thought about the 5.9 million children under 5 who die every year from preventable causes.

I had the skills. I had the platform. I had the AI. What I didn't have was an excuse.

**Long Version (Full Interview):**
"Let me tell you why FOR THE KIDS exists, and why it had to exist.

I've been building software for 20 years. I've had wins and losses. I've chased Silicon Valley dreams and watched them evaporate. By 2024, I was working on yet another AI project�good tech, decent market, the usual startup grind.

Then Gospel V1.3 happened. That was my first attempt at doing something meaningful: 60% to kids, 30% to infrastructure, 20% to me. It felt radical at the time. My peers thought I was crazy. 'You're leaving money on the table,' they said.

But 60% bothered me. It felt like I was negotiating with myself. Like I was saying, 'I'll help kids, but only if I get half.' That's not a mission. That's a compromise.

So on December 13th, 2025, after 14 hours of soul-searching and spreadsheet rebuilding, I launched Gospel V1.3. I moved 10% from my pocket to theirs. Now it's 60/30/10.

Here's what that means in practice: If we hit $10M in revenue, kids get $6M, infrastructure gets $3M, and I get $1M. I'm still okay. My family is still secure. But 5 million more dollars go to saving children's lives.

I didn't do this because I'm a saint. I did it because I have daughters. And every time I look at them, I see the faces of kids who didn't get their chance. Kids born in the wrong place. Kids with curable diseases that went untreated. Kids who deserved the same love and safety my daughters have.

I built FOR THE KIDS because AI is printing money for people who already have too much. Meanwhile, UNICEF operates on a budget that wouldn't cover Meta's holiday party. That's obscene.

So I built a platform where AI doesn't just make the rich richer. It saves lives. Every subscription. Every API call. Every enterprise deal. 60% to kids. Always.

This is my life's work. If I die tomorrow, the platform keeps giving. If I get hit by a bus, kids still get 60%. That's the point.

I'm not a hero. I'm a dad with coding skills who couldn't live with himself if he didn't try."

- **Impact multiplier: 36x over 5 years** (assuming 95% retention)

- Traditional: contribute $1,000 once = $850 impact

- But the AI platform ALSO generates enterprise revenue, API revenue, and referral revenue
- Total impact: **$1,000 contribution via traditional charity vs. $5,000+ over 5 years via FOR THE KIDS**

   - B2B enterprise deal: $50K/year = $30K/year to kids

4. **Compounding Network Effects**
   - Every user who refers a friend multiplies impact
   - Every API integration multiplies reach
   - Every enterprise deployment multiplies scale
