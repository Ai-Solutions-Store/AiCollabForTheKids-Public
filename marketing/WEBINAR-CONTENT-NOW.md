### SLIDE 3: The Problem (4-7 min)
**Speaker Script:**
"Let's talk about why this matters. The charitable sector does incredible work, but it faces serious challenges‚Äîand these challenges affect everyone in this room who's ever wanted to help.

Challenge number one: Lack of transparency. When you donate to most charities, you get a thank-you email and maybe an annual report. But can you track where YOUR specific dollars went? Usually not. You're taking it on faith.

Challenge number two: High overhead. The average charity spends 20-40% on administration and fundraising. Some spend even more. Again, covering costs is legitimate, but imagine if we could reduce that to 30% using automation and AI. That extra 10-20% could help a lot more kids.

Challenge number three: Difficult to verify impact. How do you know if your donation actually helped anyone? Most charities provide aggregate numbers‚Äî'we helped 10,000 children this year'‚Äîbut you can't connect your contribution to specific outcomes.

Challenge number four: Donor fatigue. People give once, feel good for a moment, then move on. There's no ongoing engagement. You're not part of a community. You're just a transaction.

And challenge number five: Fragmentation. There are thousands of pediatric charities, all doing great work, but there's no unified platform to discover them, compare them, or support multiple causes efficiently.

The result? Well-meaning people become skeptical. Giving decreases. And the kids who need help the most suffer.

We can fix this. Technology can solve every single one of these problems. And that's exactly what we're doing."

**Q25: How do I earn voting power in the DAO?**
