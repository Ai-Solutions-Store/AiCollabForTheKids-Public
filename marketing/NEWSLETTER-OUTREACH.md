Traditional CSR programs rely on promises:
- "We allocate X% of profits to charity"
- "Our mission is to give back"
- "We're committed to social impact"

But promises can be broken. Boards change. Priorities shift. Investors pressure companies to "focus on shareholder value."

There's no enforcement mechanism. It's trust-based - and trust often fails.

FOR THE KIDS, an AI services platform, takes a different approach. They've deployed a smart contract on Base Mainnet (Ethereum L2) that immutably enforces profit allocation:

No one - not the founder, not investors, not a future acquirer - can change this allocation without deploying a new contract (which would have a different address and lose all credibility).
