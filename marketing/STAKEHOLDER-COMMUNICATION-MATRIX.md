## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This matrix maps each stakeholder group to:
- How to contact them during crisis

## STAKEHOLDER GROUPS & COMMUNICATION PLANS

### GROUP 1: CUSTOMERS / USERS

**Who they are:**
- Active platform users
- People who have made payments
- Beneficiaries of services/products
- Contributors to charity (indirectly)

**Primary concern:**
- "Is the platform working?"
- "Is my data safe?"
- "Will my money still go to kids?"
- "Can I trust you?"

**Contact methods (in priority order):**
1. In-app notification / Dashboard message
2. Email to registered email address
3. SMS (if available) for Level 4 crises
4. Twitter mentions / replies
5. Support chat / customer service

**Information they need:**
- What happened (plain language)
- If it affects them specifically
- What they should do immediately
- Timeline to resolution
- Compensation (if applicable)
- Verification that 60% to kids is protected

**Tone:**
- Honest and transparent
- Empathetic to their situation
- Action-oriented (what we're doing)
- Respectful of their concerns
- Conversational, not corporate

**Response time by severity:**
- Level 1: Within 2 hours
- Level 2: Within 1 hour
- Level 3: Within 30 minutes
- Level 4: Within 15 minutes

**Key messages:**
- Your data/payment is safe
- We detected the issue (not you)
- Here's what we're doing
- Here's when it will be fixed
- Here's what you get as compensation
- The 60% to kids is guaranteed

### GROUP 5: REGULATORY AGENCIES / LEGAL

**Who they are:**
- FTC / SEC / State AG offices
- Banking regulators
- Privacy regulators (DPA)
- Law enforcement (if applicable)

**Primary concern:**
- "Is this compliant?"
- "Are customers protected?"
- "Do we need to investigate?"
- "Is restitution needed?"

**Contact methods (in priority order):**
1. Legal counsel takes lead (NOT direct contact from company)
2. Written response through attorney
3. Formal disclosure through proper channels
4. In-person meeting through legal representation

**Information they need:**
- Factual, complete disclosure
- Evidence of compliance efforts
- Timeline of discovery and response
- Preventive measures implemented
- Legal compliance assessment
- Customer protection measures

**Tone:**
- Professional and legal
- Cooperative
- Thorough and complete
- Legally compliant
- Documented

**Response time by severity:**
- All levels: ATTORNEY HANDLES (no direct company communication)
- Response: Within legal counsel's guidance

**Key messages:**
- Company is cooperative
- Customers/public are protected
- Incident is being addressed comprehensively
- Legal counsel available for inquiry
- Company maintains compliance

**DO NOT:**
- Admit fault (attorney advises)
- Make promises about compensation
- Speculate on liability
- Communicate without attorney present
- Provide documents without legal review

**Attorney Communication Template:**
```
(Handled entirely by legal counsel - no template provided)
(Attorney makes all decisions on disclosure and communication)
(Company provides only factual information to attorney)
```

**Communication channels:**
- Attorney (primary): All regulatory communication
- Written responses: Formal legal documents
- In-person meetings: With attorney present

## CRISIS COMMUNICATION APPROVAL WORKFLOW

**For each stakeholder group:**

1. **Draft message** (Communications lead)
2. **Review for accuracy** (Relevant team)
3. **Legal review** (If needed)
4. **Joshua approval** (For Level 3+ or sensitive topics)
5. **Final review** (Communications lead)
6. **Schedule delivery** (At same time across channels when possible)
7. **Execute** (Send/post)
8. **Monitor response** (Track sentiment/feedback)
9. **Follow-up** (Next scheduled update)

## FINAL PRINCIPLES

1. **Message consistency** - Same story, customized for audience
2. **Speed matters** - Respond within time window
3. **Transparency first** - Honest before perfect
4. **Proof available** - Always provide verification
5. **Mission protected** - 60% to kids is never negotiable
6. **Follow-up relentlessly** - Don't disappear after incident
7. **Learn openly** - Share what you learned
8. **Accountability counts** - Own the failure
9. **Community matters** - Treat stakeholders with respect
10. **Actions matter most** - What you do is more important than what you say
