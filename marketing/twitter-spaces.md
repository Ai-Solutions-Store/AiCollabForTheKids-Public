### Q&A RESPONSES (Anticipated Questions)

**Q: "How do you verify the charities are legitimate?"**
A: "We partner only with verified pediatric hospitals and established 501(c)(3) organizations with EIN numbers, IRS verification, and public financial records. No random GoFundMe campaigns. No unvetted charities. We're working with organizations like Shriners Children's Hospital - institutions with decades of proven impact."

**Q: "What if you get acquired? Does the 60% go away?"**
A: "The 60/30/10 split is immutable. It's in our Gospel documents, hardcoded in our backend (dao-logic.js), and planned for deployment in smart contracts. Any acquirer would have to honor it or the platform shuts down. We'd rather die than violate the mission."

**Q: "Why not just donate 60% personally instead of building a platform?"**
A: "Because individual donations don't scale. This platform can generate millions in revenue - far more than I could personally donate. By locking 60% into the business model, we create a sustainable, growing source of funding that outlives any single person's generosity."

**Q: "Can users choose which charity receives their 60%?"**
A: "Currently, allocations go to our verified partner network, distributed based on need and impact. In the future, we may allow users to vote on allocation priorities through DAO governance - but only within our pre-verified partner list. No scams, no unvetted causes."

**Q: "What happens if the platform doesn't make money?"**
A: "If there's no profit, there's no allocation. We don't take on debt to fund charity - that's financially reckless. But every dollar of profit gets split 60/30/10 automatically. Our goal is to build a profitable, sustainable business so the 60% grows every year."

**Q: "Is this tax-deductible?"**
A: "No. We're a for-profit LLC (Trash or Treasure Online Recycler LLC, EIN: 33-4655313), not a 501(c)(3). Customers are purchasing products/services at market rates - not making tax-deductible donations. The 60% to charity happens on the backend through profit allocation, not customer contributions."

**Q: "How much have you raised so far?"**
A: "We're in early launch phase. Current focus is building the user base and proving product-market fit. First $60,000 in revenue goes directly to Shriners Children's Hospital through our self-hosted site (aidoesitall.website). No platform fees, no middlemen. Every update is posted transparently in our public GitHub repos."

**Q: "Why AI governance instead of human governance?"**
A: "Humans have egos, biases, bad days, political motivations. AI agents follow the Gospel 24/7 without deviation. Claude doesn't wake up one day and decide to change the brand colors. Jules doesn't compromise on business logic because of investor pressure. Consistency at scale - that's why AI governance wins."

**Q: "What if Claude (the AI) goes down or is discontinued?"**
A: "We have redundancy: Claude (Anthropic), Jules/Gemini (Google), Grok (xAI), Perplexity. If one AI becomes unavailable, others can step in. The Gospel documents preserve all architectural decisions, brand guidelines, and mission rules - any sufficiently advanced AI could read them and continue the work. The mission outlives any single AI model."

**Q: "Can I invest?"**
A: "We're bootstrapped for now. If we raise funding in the future, it would be from impact investors or ESG-focused VCs who understand and respect the 60/30/10 split. We'd never take money from anyone trying to change the mission. Check our LinkedIn or email partnerships@aidoesitall.website if you're a serious impact investor."

## CRISIS MANAGEMENT

### Handling Trolls/Disruptors

**If someone:**
- Uses profanity: "Hey, let's keep this professional. Warning 1."
- Spreads misinformation: "Can you cite a source for that claim?"
- Derails conversation: "That's off-topic. Let's refocus on [TOPIC]."
- Violates rules after warning: Remove them from speakers, block if necessary

**If you get dogpiled:**
- Stay calm, don't engage emotionally
- Acknowledge valid criticism: "That's a fair point. Here's our position..."
- Ignore bad-faith attacks
- Pin a tweet with facts/receipts

---

### Handling Technical Issues

**If audio cuts out:**
- Apologize: "Sorry, technical issue. Can you hear me now?"
- Have co-host or moderator take over while you troubleshoot
- Switch to backup device if needed

**If Space crashes:**
- Immediately tweet: "Space crashed. Restarting in 5 minutes: [NEW LINK]"
- Start new Space, summarize what was covered
- Apologize to audience, extend Q&A time to make up for it
