**"I don't need AI services"**

**"Why not give 100% to charity then?"**
"Because they need to sustain operations (30%) and compensate the founder fairly (10%). But 60% means kids get the majority of every dollar - which is substantially more than typical corporate giving (usually 1-5%)."

**"This sounds like a marketing gimmick"**
"I understand the skepticism! That's why they use smart contract technology - it's not marketing language, it's enforceable code. The 60% happens automatically. Even the CEO can't change it."

**"I already give to charity"**
"That's awesome! This doesn't replace your giving. It's about being intentional with purchases you're already making. If you need AI services anyway, choose one where 60% helps kids. Your giving stays the same - your purchasing just does more."

**"I'll just contribute directly to charity"**
"Absolutely - direct giving is important! This is just a different model for people who need AI services. You're not contributing extra; you're choosing which company to hire. Same cost either way."

**"I don't understand blockchain/smart contracts"**
"No problem! Think of it this way: The 60% allocation is written in unbreakable code and verified by a public system. It can't be changed or reduced. It's like a legal contract that automatically enforces itself."

**"What if the company goes out of business?"**
"Then it goes out of business - but while it operates, 60% goes to kids. There's no long-term commitment required. Each purchase is independent."

**"I need to think about it"**
"Of course! Here's the website: https://aidoesitall.website. Take your time. If you end up needing AI services, we're an option. And if not, that's totally fine too."
