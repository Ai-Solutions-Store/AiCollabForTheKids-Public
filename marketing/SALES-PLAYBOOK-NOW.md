## 2. DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Goal:** Qualify the prospect, identify pain points, schedule demo
**Required Tools:** CRM (log everything), video conferencing, screen share capability

---

### Pre-Call Preparation (15 minutes)

**Research Checklist:**
- [ ] Review company website and recent news
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for decision-makers
- [ ] Identify existing AI vendors (if public)
- [ ] Note any CSR/ESG initiatives
- [ ] Review their Glassdoor/employee reviews (culture fit)
- [ ] Prepare 3 custom talking points based on their industry

---

### Opening (2 minutes)

**Script:**

> "Hi [Name], thanks for joining. I'm [Your Name] from FOR THE KIDS. Before we dive in, I want to set expectations: This isn't a sales pitch. My goal is to understand if we're a fit for your AI and ESG goals. If we're not, I'll tell you. Sound good?"

**Why this works:** Disarms defensiveness, positions you as consultant vs. salesperson.

---

### Discovery Questions (20 minutes)

**SECTION 1: Current AI Usage**

**Q1:** "Are you currently using any AI solutions in your business? Which ones?"
**Listen for:** OpenAI, Google, Azure, in-house models, "exploring AI"
**Red Flag:** "We tried AI and it didn't work" = needs education, not ready
**Green Flag:** "We use multiple AI vendors" = pain point we can solve

**Q2:** "What's working well with your current setup? What's frustrating?"
**Listen for:** Vendor lock-in complaints, cost concerns, compliance issues
**Follow-up:** "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?"

**Q3:** "How are you handling age-gating or content safety? Especially if you serve families?"
**Listen for:** COPPA concerns, manual moderation, fear of lawsuits
**Value Prop Trigger:** "We have built-in COPPA-compliant age-gating. It's automatic."

---

**SECTION 2: ESG/CSR Landscape**

**Q4:** "Does your company have ESG goals or a corporate giving program?"
**Listen for:** "Yes, we donate X% of revenue" or "Board wants us to do more"
**Red Flag:** "We don't believe in that stuff" = culture misalignment, move on
**Green Flag:** "Yes, but it's hard to track/prove impact" = perfect fit

**Q5:** "Who owns the CSR budget? Is it separate from technology spend?"
**Listen for:** Budget consolidation opportunity
**Value Prop Trigger:** "What if your AI spend automatically counted as CSR impact?"

**Q6:** "How do you currently report ESG impact to stakeholders?"
**Listen for:** Manual spreadsheets, third-party consultants (expensive)
**Value Prop Trigger:** "We generate quarterly impact reports automatically. No extra work."

---

**SECTION 3: Pain Points & Priorities**

**Q7:** "What would make your life easier in the next 6 months?"
**Listen for:** Hiring challenges, customer retention, regulatory compliance
**Note:** Map their answer to our value props

**Q8:** "If you could tell your CEO one thing about your team's impact, what would it be?"
**Listen for:** Desire to be seen as strategic, not just cost center
**Value Prop Trigger:** "Imagine telling your CEO: 'Our AI stack funded 500 pediatric treatments this quarter.'"

**Q9:** "What's your timeline for making a decision on new vendors?"
**Listen for:** "Evaluating now" (hot) vs. "Maybe next year" (nurture)
**Qualify:** Budget cycle, approval process, decision-makers involved

---

### Transition to Demo Scheduling (5 minutes)

**Script:**

> "Based on what you've shared, I think there's a strong fit here. Specifically, I heard you say [pain point 1], [pain point 2], and [pain point 3]. I'd love to show you how we solve those in a 30-minute demo.
>
> Fair warning: I'm going to show you our profit allocation model. 60% of what you pay goes to verified pediatric charities. It's baked into the platform. If that makes you uncomfortable, let's not waste each other's time. But if that excites you, you're going to love this."

**Listen for:**
- **"That's amazing!"** = schedule demo immediately
- **"How does that even work?"** = curiosity, good sign
- **"We can't do charity stuff"** = probe deeper, might be a misunderstanding
- **"I need to talk to my boss"** = add boss to demo invite

---

**Script:**

## 4. OBJECTION HANDLING (15 Common Objections)

**Framework:** Feel, Felt, Found Method + Proof

---

### OBJECTION 1: "This sounds like charity. We're a for-profit business."

**Response:**

> "I hear you. And you're right - we're NOT a charity. We're a for-profit AI platform. Here's the difference:
>
> When you buy from AWS, 100% of your payment goes to Amazon shareholders. When you buy from us, 60% goes to verified pediatric charities as a structural profit allocation. You're still buying AI services. The charity part is OUR business model, not yours.
>
> Think of it like TOMS Shoes. You buy shoes. They donate shoes. You're still just buying shoes.
>
> Does that distinction make sense?"

**Proof Point:** "We have 50+ for-profit enterprise clients. None of them classify this as a donation. It's a vendor payment."

---

### OBJECTION 2: "We already have an AI vendor (OpenAI/Google/Azure)."

**Response:**

> "Great! Most of our clients do. Here's the question: Are you locked into one model?
>
> What happens when GPT-5 isn't the best at [their use case]? Or when Google releases something better? You're stuck renegotiating contracts, retraining teams, rewriting code.
>
> We let you use ALL the major models - Claude, Gemini, Grok, Perplexity - with one API. You can switch models per task. No vendor lock-in.
>
> Plus, 60% of what you pay helps children. Your current vendor can't say that."

**Proof Point:** "We have clients who REPLACED OpenAI and clients who USE US ALONGSIDE OpenAI. Both work."

---

### OBJECTION 3: "How do I know the 60% actually goes to charity?"

**Response:**

> "That's a smart question. Here's how we prove it:
>
> 1. **Transparent Ledger:** Every transaction is logged. You get quarterly reports showing your exact allocation.
> 2. **Third-Party Verification:** We're working with [Charity Navigator / GiveWell] to audit our allocations.
> 3. **Blockchain-Ready:** We're building smart contract proof (optional, but available).
>
> Also, here's the thing: If we DIDN'T allocate 60%, we'd be committing fraud. Our entire business model would collapse. We have more to lose than you do.
>
> Want to talk to one of our existing customers about their experience?"

**Proof Point:** Share customer testimonial or connect them with a reference client.

---

### OBJECTION 4: "This is too expensive."

**Response:**

> "Compared to what? Let's do the math:
>
> Right now, you're paying [current vendor] $X/year for AI. SEPARATELY, you're probably donating $Y/year to charity (or your board WANTS you to).
>
> Total: $X + $Y.
>
> With us, you pay $X (same AI budget), but $0.60X automatically goes to charity. You're CONSOLIDATING two line items.
>
> Net result: You SAVE money and INCREASE impact. Where's the downside?"

**Proof Point:** "Our average enterprise client saves $15,000/year by consolidating AI + CSR budgets."

---

> 2. **Sample Contract:** Your legal team can review before we even talk pricing
> 3. **COPPA/FOSTA Compliance Docs:** If relevant to your industry
>
> Most legal reviews take 2 weeks. I'll check in with you next [specific date]. Does that work?"

**Proof Point:** "We've passed legal review at [name Fortune 500 client if you have one]. Happy to connect your legal team with theirs."

---

### Common Negotiation Scenarios

### Negotiation Tactics & Best Practices

#### TACTIC 1: Anchor High, Concede Slowly

**Example:**
- **Them:** "Can you do $10K/month instead of $15K?"
- **You:** "Our standard rate is $15K. I could do $14K if you prepay annually. Or $13.5K if you commit to 24 months. What works better?"

**Why It Works:** Small concessions feel like wins. They get a deal, you protect margin.

---

#### TACTIC 2: Trade, Don't Give

**Example:**
- **Them:** "Can you throw in free custom integration?"
- **You:** "I can include 20 hours of custom integration if you upgrade to Enterprise tier. Or I can scope it as a separate project at $200/hour. Which do you prefer?"

**Why It Works:** Never give without getting. Maintain value exchange.

---

#### TACTIC 3: Use Silence

**Example:**
- **You:** "The price is $15K/month."
- **Them:** "That's too high."
- **You:** [Silence. Count to 10.]
- **Them:** "What if we did $12K?"
- **You:** "I can't go that low. Best I can do is $14K with annual prepay."

**Why It Works:** Whoever speaks first after a price objection usually concedes.

---

#### TACTIC 4: "Higher Authority" Blocker

**Example:**
- **Them:** "Can you reduce the charity allocation to 40%?"
- **You:** "I don't have authority to change that. It's hardcoded in our Gospel document. Our founder and board would have to approve, and they've explicitly said it's immutable. Is there another way I can help you hit your budget?"

**Why It Works:** Takes pressure off you. Blames "the system" instead of being stubborn.

---

#### TACTIC 5: "Split the Difference" Close

**Example:**
- **Them:** "We can only do $10K/month."
- **You:** "Our floor is $15K."
- **Them:** "Meet in the middle? $12.5K?"
- **You:** "I can do $13K if you prepay 6 months. That's my absolute limit."

**Why It Works:** Feels collaborative. They "won" the negotiation.

---

5. They ask for free pilots with no commitment (tire kickers)

**How to Walk Away Professionally:**

> "I don't think we're the right fit for your needs. Here's why: [specific reason]. I'd be happy to refer you to [competitor] who might be better aligned. Thanks for your time."

**Why It Works:** Preserves relationship, protects your reputation, saves time.
