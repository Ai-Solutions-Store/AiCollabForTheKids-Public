## 1. VIRAL COEFFICIENT FUNDAMENTALS

### What Is Viral Coefficient (K)?

**Definition:**
```
K = (Referrals generated per user) × (Conversion rate of referrals)

Example:
User shares link with 10 people
3 people click (30% click-through rate)
2 people sign up (67% signup rate)
1 person converts to paid (60% conversion)

K = 1 × 0.30 × 0.67 × 0.50 = 0.10

Meaning: Each customer brings 0.10 new paying customers
K < 1 = Product dies without marketing
K = 1 = Breaks even (sustainable)
K > 1 = Exponential growth (viral)
K > 2 = Hyper-growth (rare)
```

**FOR THE KIDS Target:**
```
Target K = 1.5-2.0

Conservative scenario: K = 1.5
Optimistic scenario: K = 2.0
Stretch goal: K = 2.5
```

### Viral Loop Math

**Growth Equation:**
```
Monthly new customers = (Current users × K × Viral cycle time)

Example with K = 1.5, 7-day cycle:
Month 1: 10 customers (starting base)
Week 2: 10 + (10 × 1.5) = 25 customers
Week 3: 25 + (25 × 1.5) = 62 customers
Week 4: 62 + (62 × 1.5) = 155 customers

Total Month 1: 252 customers (25x growth)
```

**Conservative 90-Day Projection (K = 1.5):**
