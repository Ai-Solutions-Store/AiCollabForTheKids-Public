## Usage Guidelines

### Posting Strategy
1. **Timing:** Post during peak hours for each community (evenings/weekends)
2. **Engagement:** Respond to technical questions with depth, don't just drop links
3. **Tone:** Match the community vibe (technical in AI servers, mission-focused in charity servers)
4. **Follow-up:** Share updates when hitting milestones (first $1k to charity, new features, etc.)

### Community-Specific Tips

**AI/ML Servers:**
- Lead with technical architecture, not mission
- Be ready to discuss Claude orchestration patterns
- Share code snippets and system diagrams
- Engage in threads about AI DevOps and autonomous systems

**Web3/Crypto Servers:**
- Emphasize trustless verification and on-chain transparency
- Share contract addresses and transaction hashes
- Engage in DeFi and DAO governance discussions
- Be ready to discuss gas optimization and multi-chain strategy

**Charity/Nonprofit Servers:**
- Lead with impact and transparency
- Downplay technical complexity, focus on outcomes
- Share stories about partner charities
- Be humble - you're learning from experienced nonprofit folks

**Indie Hackers Servers:**
- Be transparent about revenue, costs, and challenges
- Share the "why" behind unconventional decisions
- Engage in bootstrapping and sustainability discussions
- Offer to help others build with similar models

### Red Flags to Avoid
- Don't spam multiple channels in same server
- Don't post same message across servers verbatim (customize each)
- Don't ignore questions or "hit and run"
- Don't oversell - let the tech and mission speak
- Don't violate server self-promotion rules (check pins/rules first)

### Engagement Hooks
If people engage, be ready to discuss:
- Live deployment walkthroughs (screen share the AI deploying code)
- Smart contract code review sessions
- Open office hours for indie builders
- Partnership opportunities for charities
- Technical deep-dives into AI orchestration
