## 5. CORPORATE MATCHING GIFT GUIDE

### "Double Your Impact: Corporate Matching Gifts 101"

**What is a Corporate Matching Gift Program?**

Many companies match employee charitable contributions dollar-for-dollar (or even 2:1 or 3:1). This means your $50 contribution becomes $100 (or more) for kids - automatically.

---

### STEP-BY-STEP: HOW TO GET YOUR COMPANY TO MATCH

#### STEP 1: Check if Your Company Has a Matching Program

**How to find out:**
1. Check your HR portal or intranet (search "matching gifts" or "charitable giving")
2. Ask your HR or CSR/ESG team directly
3. Use databases:
   - **Double the Donation**: https://doublethedonation.com/matching-gift-search
   - **HEP Development**: https://hepdevelopment.com/matching-gift-companies/

**Common companies with matching programs:**
- Microsoft, Google, Apple, Amazon
- Most Fortune 500 companies
- Many mid-size tech/finance/consulting firms

---

#### STEP 2: Understand Your Company's Policy

**Questions to ask HR/CSR:**
- Does our company match contributions to tech platforms with charitable missions?
- Do you match subscription fees or only direct donations?
- What is the matching ratio? (1:1, 2:1, etc.)
- Is there an annual limit per employee?
- What documentation is required?

**If they say "We only match 501(c)(3) donations":**
- FOR THE KIDS allocates 60% to verified 501(c)(3) charities
- Ask if they'll match the charitable portion of subscription fees
- Propose treating it as a "cause marketing partnership"

---

#### STEP 3: Submit Your Matching Gift Request

**What you'll need:**
- Proof of contribution (receipt, invoice, payment confirmation)
- FOR THE KIDS charitable mission documentation (Gospel V1.3, charity verification)
- Matching gift request form (from your HR/CSR team)

**Template email to HR/CSR:**

```

**Why this qualifies:**
FOR THE KIDS operates under "Gospel V1.3" - a binding commitment to allocate 60% of all revenue to verified pediatric charities including St. Jude, CHOP, Make-A-Wish, and others. The allocation is blockchain-verified and transparent.

While this is a subscription platform (not a direct donation), the majority of my payment goes directly to kids' charities. I believe this aligns with [Company Name]'s commitment to employee giving and social impact.
```

### MATCHING GIFT FAQS

**Q: Will my company match subscription fees?**
A: Some do, some don't. Position it as "charitable contribution via cause marketing platform" - emphasize the 60% allocation.

**Q: What if FOR THE KIDS isn't in their database?**
A: Provide Gospel V1.3 documentation and charity verification details. Many companies allow manual submissions for new organizations.

**Q: Can I match recurring subscriptions?**
A: Depends on company policy. Some match one-time, some match ongoing. Ask!

**Q: What if I get rejected?**
A: Escalate to CSR leadership, propose corporate partnership, or simply share with colleagues (collective impact still matters).
